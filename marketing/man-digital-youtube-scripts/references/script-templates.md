# Script Templates

## Long-form tutorial

1. **Cold open (10–20s)** — state the real business consequence of not knowing this, in one
   persona's voice (pull from
   [voice-and-positioning.md](voice-and-positioning.md) ICP pain quotes). No rhetorical
   question.
2. **What we're covering** — 3–5 plain-language bullets. No filler intro beyond a brief
   "welcome back."
3. **Why it matters** — the business reason, before any tool screen. Apply the sequencing
   principle: visibility → governed workflow → AI, in that order. Never open with a feature.
4. **Walkthrough** — screen-by-screen, one concrete action per beat. No vague "then you
   configure things."
5. **Mistakes / gotchas** — 3–5 specific failure modes, not generic "be careful" warnings.
6. **Recap** — 2–3 sentences, one idea each.
7. **CTA** — one concrete line (for example, "Send us your portal, we'll tell you what's
   broken"). No "smash that like button," no generic subscribe-begging.

## Shorts (under 60 seconds)

One point. One example. Hook in the first 3 seconds by stating the consequence, not asking a
question. No chapters, no recap section — point, then proof, then an optional one-line CTA.

## Promotional

Lead with the specific business problem and its concrete consequence for the ICP persona.
One proof point — a real number or real client outcome from the Client Case Studies file,
never invented. One clear next step. No "in today's landscape" framing, no adjective
stacking.

## Case study

Structure loosely on StoryBrand (see
[voice-and-positioning.md](voice-and-positioning.md)): the client is the hero, MAN Digital is
the guide. Problem (the client's actual before-state) → plan (what MAN Digital built) →
result (real numbers only, from the Client Case Studies file). Never fabricate a metric — if
the file has no number for a claim, describe the qualitative change instead of inventing one.

## Brain-dump transform mode

Triggered when the user pastes another video's script or transcript instead of giving a
topic. About 90% of these are tutorial screen-shares in a tool, usually HubSpot.

1. **Read the whole transcript first.** Identify the actual sequence of on-screen actions —
   what tool, what clicks, what gets shown, in what order. This sequence is the footage
   being narrated over, and it stays intact.
2. **Do not translate line-by-line.** The source video's opinions, jokes, CTAs, and
   interpretation are not MAN Digital's. Only the screen-action sequence carries over.
3. **Re-derive the angle.** For each screen beat, ask what MAN Digital would say about it
   from a RevOps/governance standpoint, using the sequencing principle (visibility →
   workflow → AI). Reframe around the business consequence, not the feature.
4. **Strip source-video artifacts**: rhetorical hooks ("Pretty cool, right?"), generic
   influencer sign-offs ("smash that like button"), and unverifiable claims about the tool.
   Replace with MAN Digital's own framing and CTA.
5. **Apply the main skill's Steps 1–6** (clarifying questions, voice grounding,
   readability, blocklist) exactly as if this were a fresh script.
