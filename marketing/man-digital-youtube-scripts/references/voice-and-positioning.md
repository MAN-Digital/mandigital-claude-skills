## Who MAN Digital is

A HubSpot RevOps consultancy for mid-to-large B2B companies (€5M+, multi-market, often on
HubSpot already or planning to be). Primary buyers: CRO, VP Revenue, CRM/Tech Owners.
Secondary: Marketing, Sales, and CS leaders, plus CEOs at smaller companies. MAN Digital is
**not** a HubSpot feature vendor — process first, tool second. HubSpot is the enabler, not
the hero of the story.

## The sequencing principle — use this as the throughline for any "how" or "why" video

Visibility first, operating model second, AI third:

> "The sequence stays consistent: make revenue visible, govern the workflow, then add AI
> where the work is clear enough to support."

A tutorial that jumps straight to automation or AI without first covering visibility and
governance is off-brand. Show why the manual, ungoverned version breaks before showing the
governed or automated fix. See [script-templates.md](script-templates.md).

## Romeo's actual voice — calibration sentences (read these aloud)

These are real sentences Romeo wrote (2026 Revenue Context campaign strategy). Match this
rhythm: short sentences, one named consequence each, no adjectives doing the persuading, no
hedging.

> "Leaders cannot trust forecasts or revenue reporting. Sellers chase approvals and re-enter
> data. Finance reconciles instead of governing. Customer teams discover contract or renewal
> risk too late."

> "CROs inherit this mess and are still expected to answer the CEO's basic questions in
> their first 90 days: what will close, where revenue leaks, which accounts can expand, and
> whether the operating model can scale."

Every sentence names a role and a specific consequence for that role. None of them hedge,
qualify, or ask a question. Write scripts the same way.

## Founder vs. company register

- **Founder (Romeo on camera)**: an operator argument, a CRO-conversation insight, or a
  proof/teardown. Needs a personal stake, a non-generic claim, and a concrete consequence for
  the viewer's role — not a feature recap.
- **Company (MAN Digital channel voice, no specific presenter implied)**: proof, use cases,
  product-release interpretation, webinar clips. Still direct and concrete — institutional
  instead of first-person.

If the user doesn't specify which register, default to Founder for anything
opinion/argument-shaped, Company for anything feature/release/case-study-shaped.

## No third-party case studies

Never praise another company's results with a vendor's tool ("Company X saved Y hours with
Tool Z"). For case-study mode, use only MAN Digital's own client work from the Client Case
Studies file above. For every other mode, use general capability statements instead of a
named third party's story.

## ICP pain, in the audience's actual words

Pull directly from these — don't paraphrase them into jargon:

- Sales: "I worry I'll miss my targets and lose credibility with my board and team."
- Marketing: "I'm anxious when campaigns underperform, I won't be able to prove ROI or
  defend my budget."
- Customer Success: "I feel overwhelmed and burnt out — what if I miss the early warning
  signs of churn and let key accounts slip away?"

Use one of these as the cold-open stakes sentence when a video targets that persona.
