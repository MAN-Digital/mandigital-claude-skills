# LinkedIn Content Formats - When to Use Which Type

## Overview

Different content types serve different purposes. Choose the format that best matches your goal and content.

---

## The 10 Standard Content Formats

### 1. Practical How-To's

**Purpose:** Teach readers how to execute something

**Format:** Step-by-step listicle

**Best For:**
- Tactical advice
- Process explanations
- Implementation guides

**Structure:**
```
[Hook establishing problem]

Here's how to [solve it]:

1. [Step 1 with brief explanation]
2. [Step 2 with brief explanation]
3. [Step 3 with brief explanation]

[Outcome if they follow]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "How to clean your HubSpot contact database in 3 steps"
- "How to set up first-touch attribution in HubSpot"
- "How to build a lead scoring model that actually works"

---

### 2. Strategic How-To's

**Purpose:** Share an opinionated stance on how to do something better

**Format:** Listicle with contrarian angle

**Best For:**
- Thought leadership
- Challenging best practices
- Strategic insights

**Structure:**
```
[Hook with contrarian take]

Most people [common approach].

Here's what works better:
• [Strategic point 1]
• [Strategic point 2]
• [Strategic point 3]

[Why this approach wins]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "Stop posting daily on LinkedIn. Do this instead."
- "Why your HubSpot implementation is backwards"
- "The attribution model everyone uses (and why it's wrong)"

---

### 3. Product-Led

**Purpose:** Show how a product/tool works and solves problems

**Format:** Feature showcase with benefits

**Best For:**
- Tool education
- Product positioning
- Use case demonstration

**Structure:**
```
[Hook establishing pain point]

[Tool name] solves this with [approach].

Here's what you can do:
• [Feature 1 → Benefit]
• [Feature 2 → Benefit]
• [Feature 3 → Benefit]

[Result/outcome]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "5 HubSpot features that save 15 hours weekly"
- "How HubSpot Workflows eliminate manual tasks"
- "The HubSpot dashboard every RevOps team needs"

---

### 4. Curations

**Purpose:** Roundup of people/tools/businesses that help readers

**Format:** Curated list with explanations

**Best For:**
- Resource sharing
- Tool recommendations
- Expert highlighting

**Structure:**
```
[Hook introducing criteria]

[Number] [category] worth knowing:

1. [Item 1] - [Why it matters]
2. [Item 2] - [Why it matters]
3. [Item 3] - [Why it matters]

[Action step]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "7 RevOps tools that punch above their price"
- "5 LinkedIn creators every B2B marketer should follow"
- "3 HubSpot integrations that transformed our workflow"

---

### 5. Thought Leadership

**Purpose:** Showcase unique perspective on industry trends/arguments

**Format:** Opinionated take with support

**Best For:**
- Industry commentary
- Trend analysis
- Contrarian views

**Structure:**
```
[Hook with bold opinion]

[Why this matters]

Here's what most people miss:
[Detailed perspective]

[Where this is heading]

[Implication for audience]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "Why 'marketing automation' is becoming 'revenue operations'"
- "The death of first-touch attribution"
- "B2B buying has fundamentally changed—and most companies haven't adapted"

---

### 6. Data-Driven Content

**Purpose:** Share results from experiments, sales, processes

**Format:** Results presentation with insights

**Best For:**
- Credibility building
- Proof of concept
- Methodology validation

**Structure:**
```
[Hook with surprising stat]

Here's what we learned:

Before:
• [Metric 1]
• [Metric 2]

After:
• [Metric 1 improved]
• [Metric 2 improved]

The approach: [Framework used]

[Key insight]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "We tracked $100M in revenue. Here's what actually drives attribution."
- "I analyzed 200+ HubSpot setups. The pattern is clear."
- "From 10% to 85% attribution accuracy in 90 days"

---

### 7. Teardowns

**Purpose:** Explain why what a brand/person is doing works (or doesn't)

**Format:** Analysis with lessons

**Best For:**
- Educational content
- Pattern recognition
- Learning from others

**Structure:**
```
[Hook introducing subject]

What they did:
[Analysis of approach]

Why it works:
• [Reason 1]
• [Reason 2]
• [Reason 3]

What you can apply:
[Actionable takeaway]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "How Slack hit $1B ARR: Their HubSpot strategy"
- "Why this cold email gets 40% response rates"
- "Deconstructing the perfect LinkedIn profile (with examples)"

---

### 8. Stories

**Purpose:** Tell a story that helps readers understand themselves and their options

**Format:** Narrative with lesson

**Best For:**
- Connection building
- Memorable lessons
- Emotional resonance

**Structure:**
```
[Hook with story opening]

[Story development with conflict]

The turning point: [Key moment]

What changed: [Resolution]

The lesson: [Insight extracted]

[How this applies to reader]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "How a 30-second conversation changed my pricing forever"
- "The client meeting that taught me everything about positioning"
- "Why I spent $50K on HubSpot before learning this lesson"

---

### 9. Rants

**Purpose:** Express frustration about something (that resonates with audience)

**Format:** Passionate critique with solution

**Best For:**
- Emotional connection
- Community building
- Calling out bad practices

**Structure:**
```
[Hook expressing frustration]

[What's wrong]

This is happening because: [Root cause]

What drives me crazy: [Specific examples]

What should happen instead: [Solution]

[Call to action or solidarity]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "Stop calling everything 'AI-powered.' Most of you are lying."
- "The HubSpot 'experts' giving dangerous advice"
- "Why most LinkedIn advice is worthless"

**⚠️ Use Carefully:**
- Must be authentic frustration
- Should resonate with audience
- Needs constructive angle
- Can alienate if overdone

---

### 10. Advice

**Purpose:** Give advice based on owned experiences

**Format:** Direct guidance from experience

**Best For:**
- Sharing lessons learned
- Mistakes to avoid
- Hard-won wisdom

**Structure:**
```
[Hook with experience marker]

Here's what I learned:

[Key advice points]

If I could redo it:
[What I'd change]

[Specific action for reader]
```

**Example Topics:**
- "What I'd tell my 25-year-old self about RevOps"
- "The HubSpot mistake that cost us $30K (and how to avoid it)"
- "5 things I wish I knew before implementing attribution"

---

## Listicle Variations

Listicles are the most common format. Here are the variations:

### Basic Listicle
- Simple numbered list
- Each item is equal weight
- Easy to scan and share

### Step-by-Step Listicle
- Sequential steps
- Must follow order
- Process-oriented

### How-To Listicle
- Problem + solution pairs
- Each item teaches something
- Actionable focus

### Lessons Listicle
- Insights extracted from experience
- Each has lesson or takeaway
- Retrospective angle

## Format Selection Guide

**Choose Based on Your Goal:**

| Goal | Recommended Format |
|------|-------------------|
| Build credibility | Data-Driven, Teardown |
| Teach tactical skill | Practical How-To |
| Share opinion | Thought Leadership, Rant |
| Tell personal story | Story, Advice |
| Show results | Data-Driven, Case Study |
| Recommend resources | Curations |
| Explain product | Product-Led |
| Challenge convention | Strategic How-To, Rant |

**Choose Based on Your Content:**

| Content Type | Best Format |
|-------------|-------------|
| Personal experience | Story, Advice |
| Research findings | Data-Driven |
| Industry observation | Thought Leadership |
| Process explanation | Practical How-To |
| Tool recommendations | Curations, Product-Led |
| Frustration with status quo | Rant |
| Analysis of others | Teardown |
| Contrarian take | Strategic How-To |

## Mixing Formats

**You can combine formats:**

**Story + Lesson**
```
[Personal story]
The lesson: [Listicle of insights]
```

**Data + How-To**
```
[Results from experiment]
Here's how to replicate:
[Step-by-step]
```

**Rant + Advice**
```
[Frustration with bad practice]
Do this instead:
[Actionable advice]
```

**Teardown + Advice**
```
[Analysis of what works]
How to apply this:
[Tactical steps]
```

## Common Format Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Wrong Format for Content**
```
❌ Using listicle for emotional story
✅ Using story format for emotional content
```

**Mistake 2: Mixing Too Many Formats**
```
❌ Story + listicle + rant + advice all in one post
✅ One primary format, possibly one secondary
```

**Mistake 3: Following Format Too Rigidly**
```
❌ "I must have exactly 5 bullets because it's a listicle"
✅ "I'll use 3-7 bullets based on what's needed"
```

**Mistake 4: No Format at All**
```
❌ Stream of consciousness without structure
✅ Clear format with intentional structure
```

## Quick Format Selector

**Answer these questions:**

1. **What's your primary goal?**
   - Teach → How-To
   - Persuade → Thought Leadership
   - Connect → Story
   - Prove → Data-Driven

2. **What's your content type?**
   - Personal → Story/Advice
   - Research → Data-Driven
   - Opinion → Thought Leadership
   - Process → How-To

3. **What's your audience preference?**
   - Tactical learners → Practical How-To
   - Strategic thinkers → Thought Leadership
   - Story seekers → Story/Advice
   - Data-driven → Data-Driven

4. **What's your experience level?**
   - Beginner → Basic Listicle
   - Intermediate → Strategic How-To, Product-Led
   - Advanced → Thought Leadership, Teardown

**Still not sure?**

Default to **Basic Listicle** - it's the most forgiving and most shareable format.

## Format and Hook Relationship

**Different formats need different hooks:**

**Listicle Format:**
```
Hook focuses on: Number + outcome
Example: "7 mistakes killing your attribution"
```

**Story Format:**
```
Hook focuses on: Transformation or moment
Example: "This 30-second conversation changed everything"
```

**Thought Leadership:**
```
Hook focuses on: Contrarian opinion
Example: "Unpopular opinion: Your attribution model is backwards"
```

**Data-Driven:**
```
Hook focuses on: Surprising stat
Example: "73% of companies waste $50K+ on underutilized HubSpot"
```

**Match your hook to your format for maximum impact.**

---

## Remember

**Format serves content, not the other way around.**

Choose the format that best delivers your message.

Don't force content into wrong format.

When in doubt, use Basic Listicle—it works for almost everything.

Master one format before adding more to your repertoire.
