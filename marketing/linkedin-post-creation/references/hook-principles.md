# Hook Principles - Erica Schneider's Core Teaching

## Foundation: Understanding Your "Why" and Your Audience's "So What"

No matter what you bring to the table, it means nothing if your audience doesn't *get who you are* and *how you help them*.

### YOUR WHY
- Why are you sharing?
- What's your end goal?
- What do you hope to change?

### YOUR AUDIENCE'S SO WHAT
- What do they care about?
- What do they need help with?
- What content types do they seek?
- Why should they listen to *you* instead of someone else?

## The 4 Foundational Hook Principles

### 1. Poke at the Pain

**Purpose:** Light up emotions and give readers something to relate to.

**Why It Works:** Poking at pain doesn't mean hurting your audience. It means you understand they're struggling with something that you can clearly identify. Showing them you get it builds trust.

You aren't talking at them, you're sympathizing with them. Once you're on that same page, people have a much easier time considering your advice/offer/product/service as the fix to end the pain.

**Examples:**

```
"Most people struggle with [thing]."

"I'd tried every prescriptive tip, and nothing worked—until…"

"Your boss is tired of being your manager."
```

**In Practice:**

```
❌ Weak Pain: "Marketing is challenging"
✅ Strong Pain: "You're spending 20 hours weekly on content that gets 10 views"

❌ Weak Pain: "Attribution is confusing"
✅ Strong Pain: "Your CEO just asked where the $100K marketing spend went. You have no answer."
```

### 2. Get Specific with Outcomes

**Purpose:** Allow readers to picture exactly what they can accomplish after they read your content.

**Why It Works:** The more specific you get, the more people can imagine doing what you describe. When you connect the dots from theory to reality, they can:
- Picture getting from A to B
- Understand the exact next steps
- Change their mindset from "I don't know" to "I'm ready to go"

Generalized advice or outcomes, on the other hand, is hard to imagine and easy to forget.

**Examples:**

```
"Never waste time hiring B-players again."

"2x conversions in 30 days."

"Use these 9 questions to save thousands."
```

**How to Get Specific:**

**Lean into quantifiable numbers:**
- ❌ Say: "I've edited many words"
- ✅ Say: "I've edited 3M+ words"

**Lean into specific problems:**
- ❌ Say: "Struggling to send good DMs?"
- ✅ Say: "Struggling with how to send DMs that generate responses on Twitter?"

**Lean into specific outcomes:**
- ❌ Say: "Write better emails"
- ✅ Say: "Overcome low open rates and 5x sales"

**Examples:**

```
Topic: Cold Emails

❌ Generic: "Write better emails"
✅ Specific: "Overcome low open rates and 5x sales"

❌ Generic: "Learn email tactics"
✅ Specific: "2x open rates with this subject line formula"

❌ Generic: "Industry professional"
✅ Specific: "Helped 100s overcome the same problem"
```

### 3. Add Credibility

**Purpose:** Act as social proof and give people a reason to trust what you're saying.

**Why It Works:** Credibility removes the question "why should I listen to you?" before it even forms. It positions you as someone worth paying attention to.

**Examples:**

```
"I've edited 3M+ words."

"We spent 5 years building systems that net us $1M ARR."

"I've spent 1,500 hours learning about pricing psychology."
```

**Notice How Specific These Are:**
- 3M+ words, not "many" words
- Net us $1M ARR, not "made tons of money"
- Spent 1,500 hours learning, not "spent a lot of time"

**Credibility Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Experience-Based**
```
"After auditing 200+ HubSpot implementations..."
"I've worked with 50+ SaaS companies..."
"In 10 years of selling, I've sourced $10M+ in revenue..."
```

**Pattern 2: Results-Based**
```
"My system generated $500K in pipeline..."
"This approach 2x'd our close rate..."
"We've saved clients $2M+ with this framework..."
```

**Pattern 3: Time-Based**
```
"I've spent 1,500 hours researching..."
"After 5 years building..."
"In 3,000+ hours of client work..."
```

**Pattern 4: Volume-Based**
```
"I've reviewed 10,000+ cold emails..."
"After analyzing 500+ LinkedIn profiles..."
"I've edited 3M+ words..."
```

### 4. Leave a Cliffhanger (Pique Interest)

**Purpose:** Build curiosity and open a loop that makes people want to read more.

**Why It Works:** When you open a loop, people can't help but wonder:
- How does this turn out?
- What happened next?
- What's the lesson?

These questions motivate them to click through to read more.

**Examples:**

```
"I knew what I needed to do."

"I used to be riddled with self-doubt, insecurity, and limited beliefs. No more."

"The truth about trolls, bullies, and haters...is less interesting than you think."
```

**How to Create Cliffhangers:**

**Pattern 1: Tease the Solution**
```
"The fix isn't what you think:"
"Here's what changed everything:"
"The answer surprised me:"
```

**Pattern 2: Open with Outcome, Close with Promise**
```
"From $0 to $100K in 6 months. Here's exactly what we did:"
"We 10x'd our close rate. The system:"
"Attribution accuracy jumped from 10% to 85%. The framework:"
```

**Pattern 3: Create Anticipation**
```
"Most people get this backwards. I did too. Until:"
"I've seen this mistake 200 times. Here's why it happens:"
"The data tells a different story:"
```

**Pattern 4: Question Without Answer**
```
"But here's what nobody talks about:"
"The real problem? Most people never find out:"
"Then something unexpected happened:"
```

**Bad vs Good Cliffhangers:**

```
❌ Bad (gives everything away):
"I increased conversions by changing my CTA button color"

✅ Good (opens loop):
"One tiny change increased conversions 47%. The surprising part:"
```

```
❌ Bad (too vague):
"You won't believe what happened next"

✅ Good (specific tease):
"This 30-second conversation changed my entire pricing strategy"
```

## Advanced Hook Principles

### Principle 5: Add a Spiky Point of View

**What Is It:** A spiky point of view, [coined by Wes Kao](https://www.weskao.com/blog/spiky-point-of-view-lets-get-a-little-controversial), is something a bit controversial that you're willing to fiercely defend.

**Why It Works:** It shows you've thought deeply about something. It proves you're the kind of expert that isn't afraid to go a bit against the grain to defend your ideas.

**Examples:**

```
"Editing isn't a cutting exercise—it's a value-adding exercise."

"Why you should let your CEO interrupt you (even if you're not done speaking):"

"It's hard, if not near impossible, to learn how to think strategically by reading a book."
```

**How to Find Your Spiky POV:**

1. **Identify common wisdom in your field**
   - What does everyone believe?
   - What do the "gurus" all say?
   - What's the conventional approach?

2. **Find where you disagree**
   - Where have you seen this fail?
   - What nuance are they missing?
   - What works differently in reality?

3. **Form your contrarian take**
   - State your opposition clearly
   - Back it with experience
   - Be willing to defend it

**Example Development:**

```
Common Wisdom: "Post every day to grow on LinkedIn"

Your Experience: Clients who post daily with no strategy see declining engagement

Your Spiky POV: "Posting every day without strategy trains the algorithm to ignore you"

Full Hook: "Want to grow on LinkedIn? Stop posting every day.

Consistency without strategy is just noise. You're training the algorithm that your content doesn't matter.

Here's how to post less but grow faster:"
```

### Principle 6: Tap into Loss Aversion

**What Is It:** The pain from loss is greater than the joy from gains. Psychologically we can't stand the potential pain of losing something that matters to us.

**Why It Works:** People are more motivated to avoid loss than to achieve gain. Loss aversion is a powerful psychological trigger.

**Examples:**

```
"LinkedIn can ban your account. Don't wait to start a newsletter."

"Your competitors are using this attribution model. Are you losing deals because of data blindness?"

"Every week without proper attribution costs you $10K in wasted spend."
```

**Loss Aversion Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Risk Statement**
```
"Without [solution], you're risking [specific loss]"
"Every day you wait, you're losing [quantified opportunity]"
"Your competitors already figured this out. You're falling behind."
```

**Pattern 2: Missed Opportunity**
```
"While you're manually [task], competitors are [better approach]"
"The companies that implement this now will dominate. The rest will struggle to catch up."
"This window closes in [timeframe]. Here's what's at stake:"
```

**Pattern 3: Current Cost**
```
"You're wasting $[amount] on [inefficiency] right now"
"Every [time period], [negative outcome] is costing you [loss]"
"That [bad process] is burning [resource] you can't get back"
```

### Principle 7: Inspire Your Readers

**What Is It:** Balance loss aversion with hope and possibility. People need to feel they can succeed.

**Why It Works:** While loss aversion is powerful, it's too intense to tap into all the time. People also need to feel hopeful and inspired.

**Examples:**

```
"My little product just hit $90k in sales."

"If time is your biggest struggle to growing on LinkedIn, this post is for you."

"I'm not afraid of imposter syndrome. I freaking love it."
```

**These hooks make people feel:**
- If you made $90k from course sales, there's hope for me too
- Time is a huge problem for me, so hopefully this can help
- Wow, maybe I should embrace imposter syndrome instead of feeling paralyzed by it

**Inspiration Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Transformation Story**
```
"From [struggling state] to [success state] in [timeframe]"
"I used to [negative], now I [positive]"
"What changed: [key insight]"
```

**Pattern 2: Possibility Statement**
```
"You don't need [seemingly required thing] to [achieve outcome]"
"If I can [achievement], you can too. Here's how:"
"This is easier than you think:"
```

**Pattern 3: Empowerment**
```
"Stop waiting for permission. Here's how to start:"
"You already have what you need. Here's the framework:"
"The only thing stopping you is [removable barrier]:"
```

## Understanding Audience Awareness Levels

### The 5 Stages of Awareness

**1. Unaware**
- They don't know they have a problem they need to solve
- Hook strategy: Education + revelation

**2. Problem Aware**
- They know they have a problem but don't know what solutions will solve it
- Hook strategy: Problem validation + solution categories

**3. Solution Aware**
- They're aware that products can solve their problem but haven't begun research
- Hook strategy: Solution education + differentiation

**4. Product Aware**
- They're researching various products or services
- Hook strategy: Comparison + proof

**5. Most Aware**
- They're ready to buy and need a final push
- Hook strategy: Urgency + specificity

**Tailoring Hooks to Awareness:**

**For Unaware:**
```
"Social writing can kickstart your side hustle (include examples of people who monetized in X time frame)"
```

**For Problem Aware:**
```
"Struggling with inconsistent LinkedIn engagement? Here's why it's happening:"
```

**For Solution Aware:**
```
"Here's how to get started building a strong personal brand on social (include what works + why)"
```

**For Product Aware:**
```
"I've tested 12 LinkedIn tools. These 3 are worth it:"
```

**For Most Aware:**
```
"Last chance to join [program] before prices increase. Here's what you get:"
```

## Gathering Audience Information

### Method 1: Speak to Them Directly (Qualitative)

Hop on calls with your target audience and ask:
- What are your biggest struggles with [topic]?
- What have you tried that hasn't worked?
- What would your ideal outcome look like?
- What words do you use to describe this problem?

Pay attention to:
- The exact words they use
- What they light up about
- What frustrates them most

### Method 2: Run Surveys or Polls (Quantitative)

Send DMs, collect emails, or run simple polls:
- "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"
- "What would you most like to learn about [topic]?"
- "What's stopping you from [desired outcome]?"

Use this data to inform:
- Topic selection
- Angle development
- Language choices

### Method 3: Follow Them and Study Posts/Comments

Watch what your audience:
- Consistently talks about
- Shares and amplifies
- Comments on with passion
- Questions or debates

Look for patterns:
- What topics get the most engagement?
- What pain points come up repeatedly?
- What solutions are they seeking?

### Method 4: Follow Who They Follow

If they consistently comment on somebody's posts:
- Read those posts
- Note what resonates
- Understand what spikes their passion
- Identify gaps you can fill

### Method 5: Share and Note Engagement

Post your own experiences and observe:
- Which posts resonate most
- Why they resonated (check comments)
- What topics get the most response
- What angle/tone works best

Save successful posts and relevant comments for future reference.

## Specificity Must Be Data-Driven

**The Problem:** Generic hooks that could apply to anything don't capture attention.

**The Solution:** Specificity that speaks directly to your ideal reader.

### How to Be Specific

**Before:**
"Done right, you'll win hearts and minds."
- What does "win hearts and minds" mean?
- Too subjective, readers must guess

**After:**
"Done right, you'll build emotional resonance, so your offer is top of mind during decision-making."
- Now I know exactly what the author means
- Tied to specific outcome

**Before:**
"Audit processes quarterly to make your business better."
- Too many ways to "make business better"
- Vague and forgettable

**After:**
"Audit processes quarterly to identify gaps for improvement, double down on what's working, and streamline workflows."
- Now I know "better" = streamlined workflows
- Specific and actionable

### Examples: Generic → Specific

**Topic: Email Marketing**

| Question | ❌ Generalized | ✅ Specific |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Why read it? | Write better emails | Overcome low open rates and 5x sales |
| What will they learn? | Killer email tactics | How to use personalization to write emails that get opened |
| How will it help? | Get more opens | 2x open rates |
| Why trust you? | Industry professional | You've helped 100s overcome the same problem |

**Topic: AI for Academia**

| Question | ❌ Generalized | ✅ Specific |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Why read it? | Prompt writing is becoming important | Prompt writing is becoming a $300,000 skill |
| What will they learn? | Prompt-writing | Prompt-writing for academia |
| How will it help? | Get better at prompts | Learn incremental prompt-writing |
| Why trust you? | Academic | Academic that experimented 100s of hours with ChatGPT |

**Topic: Team Management**

| Question | ❌ Generalized | ✅ Specific |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Why read it? | I've hired tons of people | I've hired 133 people |
| What will they learn? | How to treat your team better | How to not treat your team like children |
| How will it help? | Stop holding team back | Stop holding team back from being high performers |
| Why trust you? | Sold lots of software | Sold millions in software |

## A Note on Vanity Metrics

**Will you sometimes get tons of likes from non-targeted posts?**

Yes. These are "universal resonators"—content that achieves mass virality.

**Benefits:**
- Gain followers and attention
- Increase brand awareness
- Brand awareness can lead to more conversions

**Drawbacks:**
- May not attract your target audience or ICP
- Unengaged followers can decrease overall engagement
- Lower engagement harms post visibility and brand credibility
- Unengaged audiences don't convert

**The Dream:** Virality + value that drives both awareness AND conversions.

**The Reality:** Some of your best business comes from posts that perform "fine" (not mind-blowing likes) but speak directly to your audience, empowering them to reach out and convert.

**The Lesson:** Don't chase the dopamine monster. Chase strategic engagement.

## Examples of Good vs. Bad Hooks

### Example 1: Storytelling vs. Vague

**❌ Bad:**
```
Thread

Storytelling is becoming a buzzword.

Jet storytelling in. JET storytelling out.

We need to bring value back into focus.

How does it work?
How is it different from anecdotes?
How should businesses use it?
```

*Problems:*
- Confusing first sentence (assumes reader is on same page)
- "JET storytelling" makes no sense without context
- No clear value proposition
- Questions without answers
- No hook—doesn't capture attention

**✅ Good:**
```
Two million articles are published every day on the web.

Almost none use story properly.

Storytelling: 5 steps to be more persuasive, interesting, and memorable.

[Continue with specific steps]
```

*Why it works:*
- Specific data point (2 million)
- Clear problem (almost none use story properly)
- Specific outcome (persuasive, interesting, memorable)
- Sets up valuable information

### Example 2: Generic vs. Specific

**❌ Bad:**
```
Start up fundraising is hard.

In the past two years I spent hundreds of hours talking to founders creating decks and pitching.

These five things would have cut that time in half.
```

*Problems:*
- "Fundraising is hard" is vague—doesn't specify why
- No clear outcome from cutting time in half
- Missing emotional connection

**✅ Good:**
```
Start up fundraising is hard.

It can drain your energy and kill your confidence.

In the past two years I spent hundreds of hours talking to founders, creating decks, and pitching.

These five things would have cut that time in half and got me funded faster.
```

*Why it works:*
- Specific pain (drain energy, kill confidence)
- Clear outcome (funded faster)
- Emotional connection to frustration

### Example 3: Vague vs. Specific with Credibility

**❌ Bad:**
```
Most people think conflict is a bad thing but avoiding conflict can be worse.

I know I'm a recovering conflict avoider.

Over the last decade I've been on a mission to embrace conflict.

Here are ten things I've learned.
```

*Problems:*
- Doesn't explain why avoiding conflict is worse
- No credibility (what makes you qualified?)
- Vague outcome ("ten things" could be anything)

**✅ Good:**
```
Most people think conflict is a bad thing.

As a recovering conflict avoider, I know it's easier to run than resolve.

But avoiding it can ruin relationships with yourself and others.

Ten lessons from ten plus years facing my fears and finding my voice.
```

*Why it works:*
- Specific pain (easier to run than resolve)
- Clear consequence (ruin relationships)
- Credibility (10+ years)
- Specific outcome (face fears, find voice)

## Practical Hook Development Exercise

**Step 1: Identify Your Topic**
- What's the main idea you want to share?

**Step 2: Apply the 4 Principles**

**Poke at the Pain:**
- What's the specific struggle?
- How does it feel?
- What's the real impact?

**Get Specific:**
- What's the quantified problem?
- What's the measurable outcome?
- What's the exact benefit?

**Add Credibility:**
- What's your experience?
- What are your results?
- What makes you qualified?

**Leave a Cliffhanger:**
- How can you tease the solution?
- What can you hold back?
- How do you create curiosity?

**Step 3: Write 3-5 Variations**

**Step 4: Test Each Hook**
- Would you stop scrolling for this?
- Is it specific enough?
- Does it have credibility?
- Does it open a loop?

**Step 5: Refine and Select**
- Choose the strongest 1-2
- Polish the wording
- Test mobile preview

Master the principles. Apply them consistently. Watch your engagement transform.
