# LinkedIn Post Workbook - Complete Workflow

## Purpose

This document provides the complete step-by-step workflow for turning seed content into high-performing LinkedIn posts. Follow these steps after you have your seed content ready.

---

## Workflow Overview

```
Seed Content
    ↓
Reverse Outline (extract structure & nuggets)
    ↓
The Whole - Y+U+I Analysis (why, for you, for me)
    ↓
The Parts (face value, truth, assumptions, consequences, story)
    ↓
Hook Creation (4-5 per nugget using TAS/PAS/SCQA)
    ↓
Hook Selection (choose 2 best)
    ↓
Post Creation (build full posts around selected hooks)
    ↓
Template Application (structure & format)
    ↓
Final Editing & Polish
```

---

## Step 1: Reverse Outline

**Purpose:** Extract the structure and key points from your seed content.

**Process:**

1. Read through your seed content completely
2. Identify main themes/sections
3. Extract key points from each section
4. Note supporting evidence
5. Identify 3-5 "golden nuggets" worth developing

**Reverse Outline Template:**

```
SEED CONTENT OVERVIEW:
Main topic: [Topic]
Key themes: [Theme 1], [Theme 2], [Theme 3]

SECTION 1: [Theme/Section Name]
├── Key Point 1: [Specific insight]
│   └── Supporting evidence: [Stat/example/source]
├── Key Point 2: [Specific insight]
│   └── Supporting evidence: [Stat/example/source]
└── Key Point 3: [Specific insight]
    └── Supporting evidence: [Stat/example/source]

SECTION 2: [Theme/Section Name]
├── Key Point 1: [Specific insight]
│   └── Supporting evidence: [Stat/example/source]
├── Key Point 2: [Specific insight]
│   └── Supporting evidence: [Stat/example/source]

GOLDEN NUGGETS IDENTIFIED:
1. [Nugget with strong hook potential - why it's compelling]
2. [Nugget with counterintuitive angle - what makes it unexpected]
3. [Nugget with practical value - how it helps the audience]
4. [Nugget with emotional resonance - what pain it addresses]
5. [Nugget with credibility factor - what makes you qualified to share]
```

**AI Prompt for Reverse Outline:**

```
I have seed content about [TOPIC]. Please create a reverse outline that:

1. Identifies the main themes and sections
2. Extracts key points from each section
3. Notes supporting evidence for each point
4. Identifies 3-5 "golden nuggets" - insights with strong post potential

Seed content:
[PASTE YOUR SEED CONTENT]

Format the output as a structured outline showing themes, key points, and evidence.
```

**Evaluation Criteria for Golden Nuggets:**

For each potential nugget, ask:
- [ ] Is it specific enough? (not generic or obvious)
- [ ] Is it contrarian or unexpected? (challenges status quo)
- [ ] Is it actionable? (readers can apply it)
- [ ] Is it relevant to target audience? (solves their problem)
- [ ] Can it stand alone as a full post? (complete idea)
- [ ] Does it have hook potential? (can open with this)

**Output:** List of 3-5 golden nuggets ready for deeper analysis

## Step 2: The Whole - Y+U+I Analysis

**Purpose:** Deep dive into WHY, FOR YOU (audience), and FOR ME (your angle) to extract compelling angles.

**For Each Golden Nugget:**

### WHY (The Whole - Big Picture)

Questions to ask:
- Why does this matter in the grand scheme?
- Why is this happening right now?
- Why should anyone care about this?
- Why is the current approach failing?
- Why does this challenge conventional thinking?
- Why is this trend/shift important?

### FOR YOU (The Audience)

Questions to ask:
- Why is this specifically relevant to YOUR audience?
- What pain does this solve for THEM?
- What opportunity does this create for THEM?
- What risk does this help THEM avoid?
- What outcome can THEY realistically achieve?
- What's in it for THEM specifically?

### FOR ME (Your Unique Angle)

Questions to ask:
- Why am I uniquely qualified to share this?
- What's my specific insight or experience?
- What credibility do I bring to this topic?
- What contrarian view do I have?
- What story can only I tell?
- What perspective am I bringing?

**Y+U+I Template:**

```
NUGGET: [Statement of the golden nugget]

WHY (The Whole):
- [Big picture reason 1]
- [Big picture reason 2]
- [Trend or market shift driving this]
- [Why the timing is right now]

FOR YOU (Audience Pain/Opportunity):
- [Specific pain point they face]
- [Outcome they desperately want]
- [Risk they're currently facing]
- [Opportunity they're missing]

FOR ME (Your Unique Angle):
- [Your credibility/experience]
- [Your unique insight]
- [Your contrarian POV]
- [Your personal story/example]

REFINED ANGLE:
[The specific angle to take in the post that combines all three]
```

**AI Prompt for Y+U+I Analysis:**

```
I have a golden nugget: "[NUGGET]"

Please analyze this through three lenses:

1. WHY (The Whole): Why does this matter? Why now? Why challenge status quo?

2. FOR YOU: Why is this relevant to [TARGET AUDIENCE]? What pain does it solve? What outcome can they achieve?

3. FOR ME: What unique angle can I bring? (Context: [YOUR BACKGROUND/EXPERIENCE])

Then suggest a refined angle that combines all three perspectives into a compelling post angle.
```

**Example Y+U+I Analysis:**

```
NUGGET: "Most SaaS companies implement HubSpot features before cleaning their data"

WHY (The Whole):
- Software purchases are at all-time highs but ROI is dropping
- Companies chase features without foundation
- Industry shift toward "data-first" approaches
- Growing realization that automation amplifies bad data

FOR YOU (Audience = SaaS RevOps leaders):
- Wasting $50K+ annually on underutilized HubSpot
- Making decisions on unreliable attribution data
- Spending hours cleaning up broken workflows
- Competitors gaining advantage with better data

FOR ME (Your Angle):
- Audited 200+ HubSpot implementations
- See this pattern repeatedly across company sizes
- Can quantify the cost/impact with real numbers
- Have the "data hygiene first" framework

REFINED ANGLE:
"After auditing 200+ HubSpot implementations, I've found the #1 reason SaaS scaleups waste their $50K+ annual investment: They build automation on dirty data. Here's the backwards approach that actually works."
```

**Output:** Refined angles for 2-3 best nuggets

## Step 3: The Parts Analysis

**Purpose:** Zoom in and zoom out on each nugget to extract maximum depth, context, and storytelling elements.

**For Each Nugget with Strong Angle:**

### Face Value
- What's the surface-level understanding?
- What do most people think this means?
- What's the obvious/conventional interpretation?

### Truth
- What's actually true about this?
- What's the reality beyond face value?
- What's the counterintuitive truth?
- What are people missing?

### Assumption
- What assumptions are people making?
- What do they take for granted?
- What conventional wisdom is actually wrong?
- What unquestioned beliefs drive their behavior?

### Consequences
- What happens if you ignore this?
- What's the real cost of the status quo?
- What opportunity is being missed?
- What's the worst-case scenario?

### Story
- Is there a personal story that illustrates this?
- Is there a case study or specific example?
- Is there a moment of realization?
- Is there a before/after transformation?

### Zoom In (Specific Tactical Details)
- What's a micro-example?
- What's a specific instance or scenario?
- What's a tactical detail someone can use today?
- What's one small thing that makes a big difference?

### Zoom Out (Big Picture Context)
- How does this connect to larger industry trends?
- What's the broader implication?
- What's the industry-level or market-level impact?
- Where is this heading in the future?

**Parts Analysis Template:**

```
NUGGET: [Statement]
ANGLE (from Y+U+I): [Refined angle]

FACE VALUE:
What it appears to be: [Surface-level interpretation]

TRUTH:
What it actually is: [Deeper reality]

ASSUMPTION:
What people wrongly believe: [Common misconception]
Why this assumption exists: [Root cause]

CONSEQUENCES:
If ignored: [Negative outcome 1]
If ignored: [Negative outcome 2]
Opportunity missed: [What they could achieve instead]

STORY:
Personal example: [Your experience/observation]
Client example: [Case study if relevant]
Industry example: [Broader pattern]

ZOOM IN (Specific):
Tactical detail: [Micro-action or specific example]
What to do today: [Immediate step]

ZOOM OUT (Big Picture):
Industry trend: [Macro context]
Future implication: [Where this is heading]

POST BUILDING BLOCKS:
Hook potential: [How to open the post]
Body structure: [How to develop the middle]
CTA idea: [How to close/engage]
```

**AI Prompt for Parts Analysis:**

```
I have a nugget with a refined angle:

NUGGET: "[NUGGET]"
ANGLE: "[REFINED ANGLE FROM Y+U+I]"

Please analyze this through "The Parts":

1. Face Value: What's the surface-level understanding most people have?
2. Truth: What's the deeper reality they're missing?
3. Assumption: What wrong assumptions are people making?
4. Consequences: What happens if they ignore this?
5. Story: What's a specific example or story that illustrates this?
6. Zoom In: What's a tactical, specific detail?
7. Zoom Out: What's the bigger industry trend or implication?

Then suggest how to use these parts to build a compelling post.
```

**Example Parts Analysis:**

```
NUGGET: "Most SaaS companies implement HubSpot features before cleaning their data"

ANGLE: "After auditing 200+ implementations, the #1 reason scaleups waste $50K+ is building automation on dirty data"

FACE VALUE:
Most people think: "More HubSpot features = better results"

TRUTH:
Reality: Complex workflows built on bad data break more than they help
The issue: You're automating incorrect processes

ASSUMPTION:
Wrong belief: "We can clean data as we go"
Wrong belief: "Features matter more than foundation"

CONSEQUENCES:
If ignored: $50K+ annual spend with minimal ROI
If ignored: Revenue teams making decisions without proper data
If ignored: Competitors gaining advantage with better attribution
Opportunity missed: 4x ROI when foundation is built correctly

Client example: "Recent scaleup came to us spending $100K annually on HubSpot. Complex workflows. Beautiful dashboards. Zero attribution clarity. The problem? Every workflow was built on contacts with missing or incorrect data. We spent 90 days cleaning first, then rebuilt 3 simple workflows. Result: 300% improvement in attribution accuracy."

ZOOM IN (Specific):
Tactical: "Start with one simple audit - export all contacts, check for missing company data and duplicate records. Most clients find 30-40% data quality issues."

ZOOM OUT (Big Picture):
Trend: Industry moving from "feature-first" to "data-first" approaches
Future: Companies with clean data foundations will dominate attribution
Shift: From "implement everything" to "implement correctly"

POST BUILDING BLOCKS:
Hook: Contrarian take on backwards implementation
Body: The audit story + consequences + tactical fix
CTA: "What's your biggest HubSpot challenge?"
```

**Output:** Deep insights with storytelling elements ready for hook creation

## Step 4: Hook Creation

**Purpose:** Create 4-5 compelling hooks per nugget using proven frameworks.

**The 3 Core Frameworks:**

### TAS Framework (Thesis, Antithesis, Synthesis)

**Structure:**
- **Thesis:** Present an opinion or bold claim
- **Antithesis:** Recognize a barrier or counterargument
- **Synthesis:** Share a way through

**Prompt Template for TAS:**

```
Act as an expert ghostwriter. For the following nugget and angle, write 3 versions using the TAS framework.

NUGGET: [Your nugget]
ANGLE: [Your refined angle]
PARTS INSIGHTS: [Key insights from Parts analysis]

Format: Output only what is requested. Always place two line breaks between each sentence. Label as Hook 1, Hook 2, Hook 3 (no component labels).

Write in an actionable, relatable style. Short-medium sentences. Professional B2B tone. NO emojis.
```

### PAS Framework (Problem, Agitate, Solution)

**Structure:**
- **Problem:** Identify the challenge upfront
- **Agitate:** Poke the pain to increase relatability
- **Solution:** Present your approach

**Prompt Template for PAS:**

```
Act as an expert ghostwriter. For the following nugget and angle, write 3 versions using the PAS framework.

NUGGET: [Your nugget]
ANGLE: [Your refined angle]
PARTS INSIGHTS: [Key insights from Parts analysis]

Format: Output only what is requested. Always place two line breaks between each sentence. Label as Hook 1, Hook 2, Hook 3 (no component labels).

Tone: Genuine, passionate, persuasive. Register: Casual-professional. Style: Direct, personal. NO emojis.
```

### SCQA Framework (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer)

**Structure:**
- **Situation:** Set the stage (current state)
- **Complication:** The challenge this presents
- **Question:** Bridge between problem and solution
- **Answer:** The resolution (tease the solution)

**Prompt Template for SCQA:**

```
Act as an expert ghostwriter. For the following nugget and angle, write 3 versions using the SCQA framework.

NUGGET: [Your nugget]
ANGLE: [Your refined angle]
PARTS INSIGHTS: [Key insights from Parts analysis]

Format: Output only what is requested. Always place two line breaks between each sentence. Label as Hook 1, Hook 2, Hook 3 (no component labels).

Write in an actionable, relatable style avoiding jargon. Short-medium sentences. NO emojis.
```

**Hook Creation Process:**

```
FOR EACH SELECTED NUGGET:

1. Create TAS Hook (Version 1)
2. Create TAS Hook (Version 2 - different angle)
3. Create PAS Hook
4. Create SCQA Hook
5. Create 1 creative/hybrid hook

TOTAL: 4-5 hooks per nugget
```

**Hook Quality Checklist:**

Rate each hook on these criteria:
- [ ] **Specific** - Uses numbers, names, details (not generic)
- [ ] **Contrarian** - Unexpected angle or challenges status quo
- [ ] **Emotional** - Creates a feeling (curiosity, urgency, surprise)
- [ ] **Relatable** - Audience sees themselves in it
- [ ] **Data-driven** - Includes credibility markers when relevant
- [ ] **Cliffhanger** - Opens a loop or makes you want more
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly** - Scans well on phone screen
- [ ] **Authentic** - Sounds like you, not a template

**Example Hook Creation:**

```
NUGGET: "Most SaaS companies implement HubSpot features before cleaning their data"

ANGLE: "After 200+ audits, #1 reason scaleups waste $50K+ is building automation on dirty data"

HOOK 1 (TAS):
Most SaaS scaleups think more HubSpot features = better results.

But I've audited 200+ instances.

Complex workflows built on dirty data break more than they help.

The companies getting ROI do one thing first:

HOOK 2 (TAS - different angle):
Unpopular opinion: Your HubSpot implementation is backwards.

Everyone builds workflows first, cleans data later.

After 200+ audits: it should be the opposite.

Here's why:

HOOK 3 (PAS):
73% of SaaS scaleups are underutilizing their HubSpot investment.

That's $50K+ annual spend with minimal ROI.

Revenue teams making decisions without proper data.

The fix starts with cleaning your data first:

HOOK 4 (SCQA):
HubSpot should give your SaaS company revenue clarity.

But most implementations become data swamps instead.

Dirty data → broken workflows → wrong decisions.

The solution isn't more features.

It's this:

HOOK 5 (Creative):
I've seen the same HubSpot mistake 200 times.

Smart teams spending $50K+ yearly.

Building complex workflows.

Getting zero attribution clarity.

The problem? They're building on quicksand:
```

**Hook Selection Process:**

1. Create all hooks (4-5 per nugget)
2. Apply quality checklist to each
3. Score each hook (1-10) on stop-scroll power
4. Test: Would YOU stop scrolling for this?
5. Select 2 absolute best across all nuggets
6. These become your 2 post foundations

**Output:** 2 selected hooks ready for full post development

## Step 5: Post Creation

**Build complete posts around your 2 selected hooks.**

**Choose Post Structure:**

Match structure to content type:
- **Listicle** → Tactical how-to, mistakes, lessons, frameworks
- **Story** → Personal journey, case studies, transformations
- **Thought Leadership** → Contrarian takes, predictions, industry commentary
- **Data-Driven** → Research findings, experiments, results
- **Framework/System** → Methodologies, processes, step-by-step
- **Rant** → Calling out bad practices, frustrations
- **Advice** → Direct guidance, what to do/avoid

**Post Building Template:**

```
HOOK:
[Your selected hook]

CONTEXT/SETUP (2-3 sentences):
[Expand on the hook]
[Provide necessary background]
[Set up the main content]

MAIN CONTENT:
[Use insights from Parts analysis]

Option 1 - List Format:
Here's what I've learned:
• [Point 1]
• [Point 2]
• [Point 3]
• [Point 4]
• [Point 5]

Option 2 - Story Format:
[Story development]

The turning point: [Key moment]

What changed: [Result/lesson]

Option 3 - Problem-Solution Format:
The problem: [Issue]

Why it happens: [Cause]

What to do instead:
[Solution approach]

CALL TO ACTION:
[Question to spark comments]
OR
[Invitation to share experiences]
OR
[Prompt for engagement]
```

**Content Flow Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Problem → Insight → Solution**
```
Hook (establish problem with specifics)

[Space]

Context (why this matters right now)

[Space]

The insight:
• [Key point 1 with evidence]
• [Key point 2 with evidence]
• [Key point 3 with evidence]

[Space]

How to apply this:
[2-3 sentences of actionable guidance]

[Space]

What's been your experience with [topic]?
```

**Pattern 2: Contrarian → Evidence → Recommendation**
```
Hook (contrarian take)

[Space]

Why most people get this wrong:
[2-3 sentences explaining the common mistake]

[Space]

Here's what the data shows:
• [Evidence point 1]
• [Evidence point 2]
• [Evidence point 3]

[Space]

What to do instead:
[Recommended approach in 2-3 sentences]

[Space]

Agree or disagree? Let me know below.
```

**Pattern 3: Story → Lesson → Application**
```
Hook (story opening)

[Space]

What happened:
[Story development - 3-4 sentences]

[Space]

The lesson:
[Key insight extracted]

[Space]

How this applies to you:
• [Application 1]
• [Application 2]
• [Application 3]

[Space]

What would you do in this situation?
```

**Content Length Guidelines:**
- Total post: 150-250 words (ideal sweet spot)
- Hook: 2-4 sentences (20-40 words)
- Body: 80-150 words
- Bullets: 5-10 points maximum
- CTA: 1-2 sentences

**Output:** 2 complete post drafts ready for formatting

## Step 6: Template Application & Final Polish

**Apply mobile-first formatting to both posts.**

**Formatting Checklist:**
- [ ] One sentence per line
- [ ] Blank line between each sentence
- [ ] No paragraphs (2+ sentences together)
- [ ] Sentences under 15 words when possible
- [ ] Bullets for all lists
- [ ] NO emojis anywhere
- [ ] Bold used sparingly (1-2 times max)
- [ ] Scans well on mobile preview

**Then apply 11-point editing framework** (see editing-framework.md)
