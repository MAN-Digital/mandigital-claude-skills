# nRPmP Anatomy - Signal-To-Revenue Engine

Source: `/Users/romeoman/Documents/Marketing/Design/Pencil/Playground.pen`

Top node: `nRPmP`, frame name `The signal-to-revenue-engine`, 1190 x 1684, dark Medium Blue background `#000FC4`.

## Composite Role

Use `nRPmP` when the graphic needs to show a full revenue-system story: signals enter the system, HubSpot sits at the center, data and AI processing move around the loop, and the output becomes measurable pipeline or revenue. It is best for article lead graphics, LinkedIn 4:5 explainers, and blog inline posters about signal-led GTM, HubSpot RevOps, AI routing, account scoring, and revenue operating systems.

This is not a single flat component. It is a composite made from reusable subcomponents: title block, flywheel, orbit step groups, input-card pills, sequential outcome strip, closing statement, and background decorations.

## Title Block: `acPUD`

`acPUD` is a vertical stack, about 730 px wide, positioned near the top of the poster. It contains:

- `P0mBw`: Montserrat 63, bold, white, centered title.
- `DZGSn`: Lato 24, white, centered subtitle with 1.4 line height.

Use this component for high-impact top copy on dark MAN Digital graphics. Keep the title short and use the subtitle for context. Do not turn the title block into paragraph copy.

## Flywheel System: `kC04H`

`kC04H` is the primary system diagram. It combines orbit rings, eight satellite step labels, gradient arcs, arrow paths, and a central HubSpot hub.

Use it for:

- Circular lifecycle graphics.
- Signal loops.
- RevOps engines.
- HubSpot-centered operating models.
- "From signal to revenue" diagrams.

Avoid using it for:

- Simple lists.
- Strictly sequential processes.
- Dense taxonomies where a card grid would be clearer.

### Center Hub: `i5rrd`

`i5rrd` is the central platform label inside the flywheel. It contains the HubSpot logo and the copy:

- `Signal-to-Revenue`
- `Engine`
- `From signals. To pipeline. To revenue.`

Use it when the diagram needs a named operating platform at the center. Replace the center label only with another short platform/system name. Keep the hub dominant and centered inside the orbit.

`wgwpV` is the left white card titled "Signal Inputs". It contains stacked rounded pills such as:

## Closing Statement: `vlJgV`

`vlJgV` is the bottom thesis line and underline. Use it when a diagram needs a final claim after the visual system. Good replacement lines should be direct and strategic, for example:

- "Signals become revenue only when the workflow is connected."
- "Intent matters when it reaches the right team at the right time."
- "The engine is the system between signal and sale."

Keep the statement under two lines.

## Reuse Rules

- Clone `nRPmP` when the desired output is a complete poster.
- Clone `kC04H` when the desired output is a flywheel or signal engine.
- Clone `VSVo4` when the desired output is a sequential process or "right X" outcome chain.
- Clone `wgwpV` when the desired output needs input categories or signal-source pills.
- Clone individual step frames only when you need a matching orbit label. Keep the Streamline icon style intact.
- Keep the blue/orange orbit accents restrained. The orange stroke is an accent, not a new theme.
- Screenshot after edits. The orbit and process-strip connectors are absolute-positioned and can drift if resized casually.

## Coverage Boundary

The skill documents semantic, reusable components from `nRPmP`: groups, frames, cards, pills, icon frames, orbit markers, connector groups, and decorations. It does not create a separate prose file for every raw vector path inside an icon, dot, logo glyph, or ellipse because those primitives are not useful standalone components. When changing an icon itself, inspect the full child path list with Pencil before editing.
